# Client Presentation Script: travelnextlvl

*Use this script structure to present the initial homepage concepts, copy drafts, or final design prototypes to your client. Frame your work based on conversions, not subjective opinions.*

---

## 1. The Opening Setup (Setting expectations)
* **Goal:** Remind the client of their goals so they critique the site based on business effectiveness, not personal color preferences.
* **Talking Points:**
  > "Before we look at the designs, let’s quickly align on the goals we set during our discovery call:
  > 1. Your ideal client is [Target Audience Name].
  > 2. The primary metric we want to drive is [e.g., booked calls, phone dials].
  > 3. We agreed that the website should feel [e.g., premium, luxury, trustworthy] to separate you from [Competitor A] and [Competitor B].
  > Let's look at how the design system and copy structure solve these objectives."

---

## 2. Concept Presentation (Why it works)
* **Design Theme:** Explain the visual preset and art direction.
  > "For this project, we went with a [Preset Name] style. We used [Color Palette details] because it evokes [Brand Feeling]. Instead of standard stock grids, we created [Visual elements/staggers] to give the brand a custom, high-end feel."
* **Copywriting Angle:** Explain the core messaging focus.
  > "In the copywriting, we deliberately avoided phrases like 'we are passionate.' Instead, we lead with a strong promise: **[Insert Hero Headline]**. This handles the customer's main concern immediately."

---

## 3. Walkthrough Flow (Section-by-section)
* **Hero Section:** "This hooks the user instantly..."
* **Problem Section:** "We introduce the paint points early on to make them feel understood..."
* **Process Section:** "This simplifies the journey, removing buying friction..."
* **Testimonials:** "Social proof builds authority..."

---

## 4. Revisions Agreement (Closing the review)
* **Goal:** Avoid endless tweak cycles.
* **Closing Prompt:**
  > "I’ll send over this staging URL and the copywriting document. Please review it and provide consolidated feedback. 
  > When reviewing, focus on:
  > 1. *Is the copywriting accurate to your processes?*
  > 2. *Does the tone match your business personality?*
  > 3. *Does the page flow feel clear and easy to navigate?*
  > Once we consolidate your notes, we'll implement the final polish."
