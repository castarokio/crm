2. Main Business Rules the CRM Must Enforce
The CRM must be built around the following business rules.
2.1 Callers Do Not Automatically Close Deals
At the beginning, callers are mainly appointment setters and lead qualifiers.
Callers can:
Contact leads
Explain the basic offer
Qualify interest
Book calls or meetings
Send approved demo links
Add notes
Follow up
Move interested leads into the deal pipeline
Callers cannot:
Set final prices alone
Promise final delivery dates alone
Promise free maintenance

2.2 Client Payment Defines a Closed Deal
A lead is not closed because the client says “yes.”
A deal becomes closed only when client payment is confirmed.
The CRM must clearly distinguish between:
Interested lead
Meeting booked
Proposal sent
Waiting deposit
Deposit paid
In production
Waiting final payment
Closed won
Closed lost
2.3 Commission Is Paid Only From Received Money
Caller commission must be calculated only from money actually received from the client.
If the client pays in two parts, commission is paid in two parts.
Example:
Client project price: 80,000 DZD
Client pays deposit: 40,000 DZD
Caller commission rate: 20%
Caller receives: 8,000 DZD
If the client never pays the remaining 40,000 DZD, the caller does not receive commission from money that
WEB-OS never received.

Default ownership protection:
60 days from valid interested lead creation
Default inactivity expiration:
14 days without caller update or follow-up

5. Lead Protection and Anti-Theft System
Lead theft is one of the biggest risks.
The CRM must be designed so callers cannot easily steal, export, or mass-copy the lead database.
5.1 No Full Database Access for Callers
Callers must never see the full master database.
They must only see:
Leads assigned to them
One active lead at a time in dialer mode
Their own follow-ups
Their own lead history
5.2 One-Lead-at-a-Time Dialer
The caller dashboard should not show a big table with all phone numbers.
Instead, use a controlled dialer flow:
Caller clicks “Get Next Lead”
CRM shows one lead card
Lead is locked for active work

Trap lead examples:

Fake test agency
Unique Instagram test profile
Email controlled by WEB-OS
Each caller batch can contain unique trap leads.
If the trap lead is contacted outside the dashboard process, the Admin can identify which caller likely leaked
or stole the lead.
