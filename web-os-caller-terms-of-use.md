# WEB-OS Caller Terms of Use

**Document Version:** 1.0  
**Business Name:** WEB-OS  
**Role Covered:** Commission-Based Caller / Appointment Setter  
**Target Market:** Travel agencies and related businesses in Algeria  
**Manager:** Hamid / WEB-OS Management  
**Effective Date:** ____________________

---

## 1. Purpose

These Terms of Use explain the rules that every caller must accept before working with WEB-OS.

The goal is to make the collaboration clear, fair, and professional.

By accepting these terms, the caller confirms that they understand:

- How their work is tracked
- How leads are owned
- How commissions are calculated
- When commissions are paid
- What they are allowed to say to clients
- What they are not allowed to do
- How client data and WEB-OS data must be protected

No caller may start calling, receive dashboard access, or claim commissions without accepting these terms.

---

## 2. Caller Role

The caller’s role is to contact assigned prospects, create interest, qualify potential clients, book calls or meetings, and log all activity inside the WEB-OS CRM dashboard.

At the beginning, the caller is not a full sales manager and is not allowed to close full deals alone unless WEB-OS gives written permission.

The caller’s main tasks are:

- Contact assigned leads
- Present WEB-OS professionally
- Explain the general website offer
- Identify interested prospects
- Ask basic qualification questions
- Book calls or meetings with WEB-OS management
- Send approved demo links or approved messages
- Log every call, message, result, note, and follow-up in the dashboard
- Follow up with prospects when needed
- Respect all WEB-OS rules

---

## 3. Commission-Based Work

This is commission-based work.

The caller is paid only when a client brought through the correct WEB-OS process pays WEB-OS.

The caller does not receive payment for:

- Calling leads only
- Sending messages only
- A client saying “I am interested”
- A client asking for details
- A meeting being booked
- A verbal promise from the client
- A client saying they will pay later

A commission becomes valid only when WEB-OS receives confirmed payment from the client.

---

## 4. Commission Rates

The standard commission structure is:

- **20%** for a valid paying client brought through the correct process
- **25%** for a strong qualified lead where the caller follows up seriously and helps move the deal forward
- **30%** only when the caller brings the client, follows the full process, helps close the deal properly, and the final price and scope are approved by WEB-OS

The 30% rate is not automatic.

WEB-OS management decides the final commission rate based on:

- Lead quality
- Caller work quality
- Follow-up quality
- Whether the caller helped close the client
- Whether the caller followed the dashboard process
- Whether the caller made any unauthorized promises
- Whether the deal was clean and not disputed

Unless WEB-OS approves otherwise, the maximum commission is 30%.

---

## 5. Commission Is Calculated From Received Money Only

Commission is calculated only from money actually received by WEB-OS.

If the client pays in parts, the caller is paid in parts.

Example:

- Project price: 80,000 DZD
- Client pays deposit: 40,000 DZD
- Caller commission rate: 20%
- Caller commission from deposit: 8,000 DZD

If the client never pays the remaining amount, the caller does not receive commission on money WEB-OS did not receive.

---

## 6. Commission Payment Timing

Commission becomes pending after WEB-OS confirms client payment.

WEB-OS aims to pay valid caller commissions within 48 hours after payment confirmation when possible.

Commission may be delayed if:

- Client payment is not confirmed
- Client payment is disputed
- Client requests a refund
- Deal ownership is disputed
- Caller behavior is under review
- The caller made unauthorized promises
- The dashboard record is incomplete
- The payment proof is not clear

---

## 7. What Counts as a Closed Deal

A deal is closed only when the client pays WEB-OS.

The following do not count as closed deals:

- The client is interested
- The client asked for a price
- The client asked for the demo
- The client booked a meeting
- The client said “send me details”
- The client verbally accepted
- The client said they will pay later

A closed deal means:

- The client paid a deposit, milestone payment, or full payment
- WEB-OS confirmed the payment
- The deal is registered correctly in the dashboard

---

## 8. Lead Ownership

Lead ownership means the caller is recognized as the person who brought the client opportunity.

A caller owns a lead only when the lead is properly registered in the dashboard.

A valid owned lead must include:

- Business name
- Phone number or social media link
- City or location if available
- Date and time of contact
- Call or message outcome
- Useful notes
- Caller name or account
- Status update in the dashboard
- Proof if available

If a caller brings a lead and the lead later becomes a paying client, the caller keeps the commission even if the client later contacts WEB-OS directly.

The dashboard is the official proof of lead ownership.

---

## 9. Ownership Protection Period

A valid owned lead is protected for the caller for **60 days**.

If the client pays within 60 days after the valid lead was created, the caller receives the commission according to the approved rate.

If the caller does not follow up, update, or take action on the lead for **14 days**, WEB-OS may review, reassign, or remove the caller’s ownership.

---

## 10. No Dashboard Record, No Guaranteed Commission

The dashboard is the official source of truth.

If a caller claims they brought a client but the lead was not recorded in the dashboard, WEB-OS cannot guarantee commission.

Screenshots, WhatsApp messages, Instagram messages, and call logs can support a claim, but they do not replace the dashboard record.

Every serious lead must be added and updated in the dashboard immediately.

---

## 11. Duplicate Leads

If two callers contact the same business, the first caller who registered a valid lead in the dashboard owns the lead.

WEB-OS may review the case if:

- The first caller did not follow up
- The first caller entered weak or fake information
- The first caller abandoned the lead
- Another caller revived the deal after inactivity
- Another caller contributed strongly to closing the client

WEB-OS may split commission in special cases if both callers contributed.

Possible split examples:

- 70% of the commission to the original lead owner and 30% to the caller who revived or helped close
- 50% / 50% if both callers contributed strongly

The final decision is made by WEB-OS management based on dashboard history and proof.

---

## 12. Dashboard Use Is Mandatory

The caller must use the WEB-OS dashboard for all work.

The caller must log:

- Calls
- Messages
- Client responses
- Interested leads
- Follow-ups
- Meetings
- Notes
- Proof
- Status changes

The caller must not work outside the dashboard or hide leads from the system.

The caller must not rely on memory, private notes, or personal messages only.

---

## 13. Required Call Notes

Every important interaction must include useful notes.

Bad notes:

- “Called”
- “Interested”
- “Maybe”
- “Later”
- “Good”

Good notes:

- “Spoke with owner. They do not have a website. Interested in a landing page. Asked for price. Callback tomorrow at 15:00.”
- “Secretary answered. Owner unavailable. Best time to call: tomorrow at 10:00.”
- “Agency has Instagram but no website. Interested in WhatsApp booking page. Meeting booked with Hamid Friday 18:00.”
- “Not interested. Said they already have a developer. Asked not to be contacted again.”

Good notes protect the caller and help WEB-OS close deals.

---

## 14. What the Caller Is Allowed to Say

The caller may explain the general WEB-OS offer.

The caller may say that WEB-OS builds professional websites for Algerian travel agencies so clients can see offers clearly, trust the agency, and contact them directly through WhatsApp, phone, social media, or forms.

The caller may mention:

- Professional website
- Mobile-friendly design
- Direct WhatsApp contact
- Displaying travel offers
- Better online presentation
- Google Maps
- Social media links
- Google reviews section if available
- Basic SEO depending on package
- Different packages depending on client needs

---

## 15. What the Caller Is Not Allowed to Promise

The caller must not promise anything that WEB-OS did not approve.

The caller must not promise:

- Final prices
- Exact delivery dates
- Free maintenance
- Unlimited revisions
- Unlimited pages
- Free future features
- Advanced features for cheap prices
- Admin dashboard unless approved
- Booking system unless approved
- Payment system unless approved
- Mobile application unless approved
- Guaranteed clients or guaranteed sales
- Number one ranking on Google
- Anything outside the agreed project scope

The caller must not say:

- “Everything is included”
- “Unlimited changes”
- “Free maintenance forever”
- “We guarantee you will get clients”
- “We can build anything for 20,000 DZD”

---

## 16. Final Price and Scope Approval

Only WEB-OS management can approve:

- Final price
- Project scope
- Payment plan
- Delivery estimate
- Included features
- Excluded features
- Revisions
- Bug-fix period
- Discounts
- Special offers

The caller may explain the basic price range, but they must not create a final agreement with the client without WEB-OS approval.

---

## 17. Client Payment Rules

The caller must never collect money personally from the client.

All client payments must go directly to WEB-OS or to a payment method approved by WEB-OS management.

The caller must not ask the client to send money to:

- The caller’s personal account
- The caller’s BaridiMob
- The caller’s CCP
- The caller’s bank account
- Any private payment method outside WEB-OS

If a caller collects money privately or tries to move payment outside WEB-OS, this is a serious violation.

---

## 18. WEB-OS Website Packages

WEB-OS website prices generally start from **20,000 DZD** and can reach **100,000 DZD or more**, depending on complexity and quality.

General package ranges:

- Starter: 20,000 DZD to 30,000 DZD
- Professional: 45,000 DZD to 70,000 DZD
- Premium: 80,000 DZD to 100,000 DZD+

These prices are general ranges, not final quotes.

Final price depends on client needs and must be approved by WEB-OS management.

---

## 19. No Free Maintenance

WEB-OS does not include free long-term maintenance unless it is clearly written in the client agreement.

After the project is delivered, any new feature, new page, new section, redesign, integration, content update, or extra work is paid separately unless it was included in the original agreement.

Callers must not promise free maintenance.

---

## 20. Extra Work Is Paid

The client receives only what is written and approved in the agreement.

Extra work may include:

- New page
- New section
- New feature
- New language
- Admin panel
- Booking system
- Payment system
- Logo design
- Copywriting
- Monthly updates
- Redesign
- Advanced SEO
- Social media management
- New integrations

Callers must not tell clients that extra work is free.

---

## 21. Revisions

WEB-OS may include limited revision rounds depending on the package.

A revision means improving or adjusting agreed work.

A revision does not mean adding new features that were not agreed.

Recommended revision structure:

- Starter package: 1 revision round
- Professional package: 2 revision rounds
- Premium package: 2 to 3 revision rounds

The final number of revisions must be confirmed by WEB-OS management.

---

## 22. Delivery Time

Delivery time depends on project complexity and client cooperation.

Approximate delivery ranges:

- Starter: 3 to 5 days
- Professional: 5 to 10 days
- Premium: 10 to 20 days or more

These are estimates, not guaranteed deadlines unless WEB-OS management confirms them.

Callers must not promise a fixed delivery date without approval.

The client may need to provide:

- Logo
- Agency name
- Phone number
- WhatsApp number
- Email
- Address
- Social media links
- Google Maps link
- Offers
- Services
- Images

- Brand preferences

## 24. Confidentiality

All WEB-OS leads, phone numbers, emails, social media accounts, scripts, notes, dashboard data, client details, internal documents, prices, and strategies are confidential.

The caller must not:

- Copy the database
- Export leads
- Sell leads
- Share leads with outsiders
- Use WEB-OS leads for personal projects
- Contact WEB-OS leads outside the system
- Share dashboard screenshots publicly
- Share client information
- Share WEB-OS scripts or documents with another company

---

## 25. Lead Protection and Non-Circumvention

The caller is not allowed to bypass WEB-OS.

The caller must not:

- Take WEB-OS leads and contact them privately
- Offer personal services to WEB-OS leads
- Move clients outside WEB-OS
- Collect payment directly
- Present themselves as the project owner
- Hide interested clients from the dashboard
- Close deals privately using WEB-OS data
- Work with WEB-OS clients outside WEB-OS

If the caller violates this rule, WEB-OS may remove their access, cancel unpaid commissions related to dishonest activity, and take further action depending on the seriousness of the violation.

---

## 26. Client Data Handling

The caller must use client data only for WEB-OS work.

Client data includes:

- Names
- Phone numbers
- Emails
- Social media accounts
- Business details
- Notes
- Conversations
- Payment information
- Project details

The caller must not save unnecessary client data outside the dashboard.

The caller must not share client data outside the WEB-OS team.

---

## 27. Do Not Contact Rule

If a lead asks not to be contacted again, the caller must mark the lead as **Do Not Contact** in the dashboard.

After a lead is marked Do Not Contact, the caller must not call or message the lead again unless WEB-OS management gives explicit permission.

Ignoring this rule may lead to removal from the team.

---

The caller must:

- Be respectful
- Speak clearly
- Stay calm
- Be honest
- Respect client time
- Use approved scripts or approved messaging direction
- Log all work correctly
- Ask WEB-OS management when unsure
- Protect WEB-OS reputation

- Insult clients
- Insult competitors
- Use pressure or manipulation
- Harass clients
- Spam clients
- Use fake identity
- Damage WEB-OS reputation

A dispute may happen about:

- Lead ownership
- Commission amount
- Commission rate
- Duplicate leads
- Payment status
- Caller behavior
- Client complaint
- Unauthorized promises

WEB-OS will review:

- Dashboard activity history
- Lead records
- Call logs
- Notes
- Proof
- Payment records
- Timeline
- Caller behavior

The dashboard history is the main source of truth.

WEB-OS management makes the final decision.

## 30. When Commission May Be Cancelled, Reduced, or Delayed

Commission may be cancelled, reduced, or delayed if:

- The client never pays
- The client payment is refunded
- The client cancels before payment
- The client payment is disputed
- The caller made false promises
- The caller gave an unapproved price
- The caller promised unapproved features
- The caller failed to log the lead
- The caller faked activity
- The caller stole or hid lead information
- The caller contacted clients privately
- The caller collected money privately
- The caller violated confidentiality
- The caller damaged WEB-OS reputation
- The deal is under investigation

Valid commissions from clean deals should still be paid.

## 31. Caller Removal

WEB-OS may remove a caller from the team if the caller:

- Ignores dashboard rules
- Lies to clients
- Fakes call logs
- Steals leads
- Shares confidential data
- Harasses clients
- Damages WEB-OS reputation
- Works privately with WEB-OS clients
- Makes unauthorized promises
- Refuses to follow the process
- Creates repeated serious disputes

If a caller is removed, WEB-OS will review pending commissions.

Commissions from honest and valid deals may still be paid.

Commissions related to dishonest, fake, stolen, or disputed activity may be cancelled.

- Confirming client payments
